About me

Background:

Born and Raised: Kathmandu, Nepal

High School: Shanghai, China

University: Boston, USA

Hobbies and Interests:

  • Food adventures: Coming from a family of great cooks and avid food lovers, I was destined to follow the same path. I really enjoy exporing new and exquisite places to eat. In Boston, I go out at least once a week to try something new. My favorite cuisines are traditional Newari foods and Thai food.

  • Active sports: As a kid, I hated watching the television and preferred to go out and play. The childhood habits stayed, and now I like to play soccer and squash whenever I get the chance. I also enjoy swimming occasionally. Going to the gym is another hobby of mine because working out makes me feel fit and ready to tackle any problem at hand.

  • Designing and Building: Aside from the fact that I appreciate great architecture due to the intricate details of design, I love product designing and working on side projects with Arduino. Please see my projects tab for further details. The projects help me expand my knowledge base and allow me to apply what I've learnt in classes.

  • Event management: I love interacting with people and working in a team to create memorable events. My attention to detail and organizing skills make me a valuable asset to a team. With each event, I strive to gain new skills, meet new people and broaden my perspective.

Why Engineering?

Throughout my childhood, I had hopes and dreams of getting an incredible job where I get to explore and travel the world. When I was 10, my father had bought a RC Helicopter for me. THe first day that I assembled it and took it out for a fly, the helicopter broke. I was very dissapointed because I had been excited for the gift. The first step was to go to Shanghai to live with my father and to pursue my high school degree. After a great two years, I decided to enroll in Boston University as a undergraduate pursuing Mechanical Engineering. I wanted to learn more about robotics and do more in that field.

In my second year, as I got more and more into programming, I got more and more interested in the electrical and computer engineering department. I liked designing and using the 3D modelling tools, however, I really liked coding with an Arduino and building a system. I, then, switched my major to Computer Engineering and decided to take a handful to Electrical Engineering courses as my technical electives.
